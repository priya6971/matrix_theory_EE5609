\section{Problem}
In right triangle ABC, right angled at $\vec{C}$, $\vec{M}$ is the mid-point of hypotenuse AB.$\vec{C}$ is joined to $\vec{M}$ and produced to a point $\vec{D}$ such that DM = CM. Point $\vec{D}$ is joined to point $\vec{B}$. Show that:
\begin{align}
a) & \quad	\triangle AMC \cong \triangle BMD \label{eq1} \\
b) & \quad \angle DBC = 90^{\circ} \\ 
c) & \quad \triangle DBC \cong \triangle ACB \\
d) & \quad CM = \frac{1}{2} AB
\end{align}
\section{Solution}

In $\triangle{ABC}$, $\vec{M}$ is midpoint of hypotenuse AB, thus 
\begin{align} \label{eq3}
    & AM = BM &
\end{align} 
and also it is given that DM = CM. To show $\triangle AMC \cong \triangle BMD$, we use
\begin{align}
& \angle AMC = \angle BMD & (VOA) \\
	& AM = BM & (from \enspace equation \enspace \ref{eq3})\\
	& CM = DM & (Given)
\end{align}
Thus, by SAS Congruency Criteria, $\triangle AMC \cong \triangle BMD$.
Now we have to show that $\angle DBC$ is right angle.
As $\vec{M}$ is the mid point of AB, so MC bisect $\angle{C}$ in the $\triangle{ABC}$,
\begin{align} \label{eq5}
    & \angle MCB = \angle MCA = 45^{\circ} & \\
    & \angle MCB = \angle DCB & \\
    & \angle DCB = 45^{\circ} &
\end{align}
Now, as we know that $\triangle AMC \cong \triangle BMD$.
Thus,
\begin{align}
    & \angle ACM = \angle BDM & \label{eq7} \\
    & \angle ACM = 45^{\circ}  & (from \enspace equation \enspace \ref{eq5}) \\
    & \angle BDM = \angle BDC = 45^{\circ} & (from \enspace equation \enspace \ref{eq7})
\end{align}
Now, in $\triangle DBC$,
\begin{align}
    & \angle CDB + \angle DBC + \angle DCB = 180^{\circ} \\
    & 45^{\circ} + 45^{\circ} + \angle DBC = 180^{\circ} \\
    & \angle DBC = 90^{\circ}
\end{align}
Also, we have to show that $\triangle DBC \cong \triangle ACB$. After proving an equation \ref{eq1}, we can say that 
$\triangle AMC \cong \triangle BMD$. Thus, 
\begin{align} \label{eq2}
    & AC = BD & 
\end{align}
To show $\triangle DBC \cong \triangle ACB$ we use
\begin{align}
	& \angle DBC = \angle ACB & (Both \enspace are \enspace right \enspace angled) \\
	& DB = AC & (from \enspace equation \enspace \ref{eq2})\\
	& BC = CB & (Common \enspace Side)
\end{align}
Thus, by SAS Congruency Criteria, $\triangle DBC \cong \triangle ACB$.
Now, we have to show 
\begin{align}
    & \quad CM = \frac{1}{2} AB
\end{align}
Now, as we already show that $\triangle DBC \cong \triangle ACB$. So, 
\begin{align}
    & \quad DC = AB & \\
    & \frac{1}{2} DC = \frac{1}{2} AB & \label{eq12}\\
    & \quad DM = CM \\
    & \quad CM = \frac{1}{2} DC & \label{eq13}
\end{align}
So from equation \ref{eq12} and \ref{eq13}, we can get
\begin{align}
    & \quad CM = \frac{1}{2} AB
\end{align}
