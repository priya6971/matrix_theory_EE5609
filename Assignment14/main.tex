\section{\textbf{Problem}}
Let $P$ be the operator on $R^2$ which projects each vector onto the x-axis, parallel to the y-axis: $P(x,y)$=$(x,0)$. Show that $P$ is linear. What is the minimal polynomial for $P$?  
\section{\textbf{Solution}}
\begin{table}[h!]
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
\hline
& \\
Given & $P$ be the operator on $R^2$, $P$ : $R^2$ $\xrightarrow{}$ $R^2$ which projects each vector onto the x-axis, parallel to\\
& the y-axis: $P(x,y)$=$(x,0)$\\
& \\
\hline
& \\
To Prove & $P$ is linear\\
& For $P$ to be linear, it should satisfy the properties mentioned in the Given section\\
& Consider two vectors $(x_1,y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$ \\
& \\
\hline
& \\
To find & Minimal Polynomial for $P$\\
& \\
\hline
& \\
Matrix & For the projection $P(x,y)$ = $(x,0)$, the matrix of linear transform is,\\
& $P(x,y)$ = $\vec{A}\myvec{x\\y}$ = $\myvec{1&0\\0&0} \myvec{x\\y}$  = $(x,0)$\\
& So, $\vec{A}$ = $\myvec{1&0\\0&0}$\\
& \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{table}
\begin{table*}[ht]
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
\hline
&\\
Proof
& Consider two vectors  $(x_1,y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$, then\\
& $P((x_1,y_1)+(x_2,y_2))$ = $P(x_1+x_2,y_1+y_2)$\\
& $P((x_1,y_1)+(x_2,y_2))$ = $P(x_1+x_2,0)$\\
& $P((x_1,y_1)+(x_2,y_2))$ = $P((x_1,0),(x_2,0))$\\
& Thus, $P$ satisfied the property of linearity.\\
& \\
& Now, consider some scalar $k$, then\\
& $P(k(x_1,y_1)) = P((kx_1,ky_1))$ \\
& $P(k(x_1,y_1)) = P((kx_1,0))$ \\
& $P(k(x_1,y_1)) = kP(x_1,0)$ \\
& \\
& Thus, using above observations we can conclude that $P$ is linear.\\
\hline
&\\
Minimal Polynomial
& $\vec{A}$ = $\myvec{1&0\\0&0}$ \\
& Characteristic Polynomial of $A$ is \\
& $\det(xI-A)$= $\left|
                \begin{array}{ccc}
                (x-1) & 0\\
                0 & (x-0)\\
                \end{array}
                \right|$\\
& $\det(xI-A)$ = $x(x-1)$\\
&\\
& Characteristic polynomial is a product of distinct linear terms and \\
& because every root of characteristic polynomial should be the\\
& root of minimal polynomial thus, it must be equal to the minimal polynomial.\\
& \\
& Thus, minimal polynomial is equal to $p(x)$ = $x(x-1)$\\
\hline
\end{tabular}
